About the Archive

A Legacy of Moving & Milestones

Archival Play isn't a traditional commercial storefront. It is a finite, living archive built to find new homes for a private collection of vintage McDonald's toys carefully kept by my parents over several decades.

Growing up, our family moved frequently around the country. In the midst of constant changes, packing boxes, and unfamiliar neighborhoods, trips to McDonald's became our anchor. It was the primary way my dad carved out intentional, dedicated time to connect with us kids. No matter what city we were passing through, that bright sign meant stability, safety, and shared laughter.

Every toy in this archive represents a snapshot of those memories. By documenting, cleaning, and sharing this collection, I hope to pass a piece of that warmth and childhood nostalgia along to you.

Project Blueprint

Beyond liquidating this family collection, this storefront serves as a functional software development portfolio piece. It is built entirely using clean, semantic code, optimized keyboard navigation, and rigorous web accessibility (a11y) guidelines to prove that modern digital experiences can be beautiful, fast, and inclusive for every user.
